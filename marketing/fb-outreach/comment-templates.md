## Comment 3 — "How do you avoid getting scammed on Marketplace?"

> A few rules that have saved me:
> 1. Reverse-image search the photos — stolen photos are the #1 tell
> 2. Be suspicious of any listing >25% under market for the category
> 3. Never pay before you see it in person; never click payment links sent in chat
>
> I also run a free Chrome extension I built called Deal Scout that flags a lot of this automatically — under-market pricing, condition mismatches between photo and description, etc. Glad to share if useful.

## Notes
- Only post in groups where the rules allow tool recommendations. When in doubt, **ask the admin first via DM** (see `dm-templates.md`).
- If a rule says "no self-promotion," skip the link mention entirely and just give the tip — comments 1, 3, and 5 still work without the link.
- Reply to the OP, don't hijack other replies.
- **Which link to use:** for now, always link to the landing page (`https://805lager.github.io/deal-scout`). It has the demo, FAQ, and a big "Add to Chrome" button that will point to the Chrome Web Store once approval lands. Once the extension is live in the store, you can swap to the direct CWS URL — but the landing page has higher trust signals (real screenshots, demo video, contact email, privacy policy), so it usually converts better even after CWS goes live.
- Space posts out — one group per day max. Posting to 5 groups in one hour is the fastest way to get flagged as spam.
- Reply to comments on your post within the first 2 hours — algorithm rewards engagement, and being responsive is what separates a "real maker" post from spam.
